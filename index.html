Average Life Expectancies categorised by different countries

Life expectancy is the estimate of the average age a person in a particular population would be when pass away. It is a metric that has been used to compare the survial and quality of life experience for populations across the world.

The choropleth map on the right shows the average Life Expectancy for different countries, with lighter green shades indicating lower averages for Life Expectancy and dark blue shades indicating higher averages for Life Expectancy. Values with no data are shaded in grey.

Countries in Africa and Asia can be seen to have more countries with wider variety of Life Expectancies, with more countries with a lower Life Expectancy belonging to the two regions.










Comparing Life Expectancy against other factors

To examine the impact of health related factors such as Body Mass Index (BMI), and Infant Death Rate on lower Life Expectancy levels, a bubble plot is shown to the left.

Life Expectancy is depicted against BMI along the axes, with the bubbles representing each country being sized as the number of Infant Deaths per 1000 population coloured by the continent the country lies within.

The correlation between BMI and Life Expectancy appears to be linear in nature, with a some countries having lower BMI averages and high average Life Expectancy.

Countries, particularly in Africa, with higher Infant Deaths and lower BMI averages are shown to have lower Life Expectancies.
