Life Expectancy



Average Life Expectancies categorised by different countries

Life expectancy is the estimate of the average age a person in a particular population would be when pass away. It is a metric that has been used to compare the survial and quality of life experience for populations across the world.

The choropleth map on the right shows the average Life Expectancy for different countries, with lighter green shades indicating lower averages for Life Expectancy and dark blue shades indicating higher averages for Life Expectancy. Values with no data are shaded in grey.

Countries in Africa and Asia can be seen to have more countries with wider variety of Life Expectancies, with more countries with a lower Life Expectancy belonging to the two regions.










Comparing Life Expectancy against other factors

To examine the impact of health related factors such as Body Mass Index (BMI), and Infant Death Rate on lower Life Expectancy levels, a bubble plot is shown to the left.

Life Expectancy is depicted against BMI along the axes, with the bubbles representing each country being sized as the number of Infant Deaths per 1000 population coloured by the continent the country lies within.

The correlation between BMI and Life Expectancy appears to be linear in nature, with a some countries having lower BMI averages and high average Life Expectancy.

Countries, particularly in Africa, with higher Infant Deaths and lower BMI averages are shown to have lower Life Expectancies.










Examining economic trends against Life Expectancy

Investigating the influence of different economic forces, such as the Employment to Population Ratio, Percentage of Internet Users and the Labour Force Participation Rate on Life Expectancy, the parallel coordinates graph is shown to the right. Each country is shown as one line on the graph coloured by its continent.

The graph shows two main trend lines: one dominated by the colour green, representing Africa and another containing the other colours, behaving in the opposite direction.

The first trend group, as defined above shows the correlation between high values for Employment to Population Ratio, low proportions of Internet Users, high proportions of Labour Force Participation Rate, and low Life expectancies.

The differences in mean Gross National Income (GNI) and and its influences

GNI is the calculated as the total domestic and foreign output claimed by residents in a country. It is calculated by the gross domestic product (GDP) of the country, plus income earned by foreign residents minus income earned in the economy by foreign residents.

The diverging bar chart shown to the left shows the difference between a country's GNI and the average GNI, calculated as $16608.27 million USD from the data.

The colours of the bars for each country are coloured by being above or below the average Life Expectancy, which is calculated as 71.1 years from the data.

The graph shows that a country that has a lower GNI per capita is more likely to have a lower Life Expectancy averages
